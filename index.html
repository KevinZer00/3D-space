Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, known for its icy surface and heart-shaped glacier.

Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, affecting tides and creating lunar phases.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun, with extreme temperature variations.

Mars

Mars, known as the Red Planet, has the tallest volcano and deepest canyon in the solar system.

Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system and rotates in the opposite direction to most planets.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with a diverse ecosystem and vast oceans.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun, known for its intense blue color and strong winds.

Uranus

Uranus is unique for its tilted rotation axis, leading to extreme seasons and a faint ring system.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, made of ice and rock, and has over 80 known moons.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a Great Red Spot that is a giant storm.

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, providing the energy that supports life on Earth.

Sirius A

Sirius A is the brightest star in our night sky, part of the binary star system with Sirius B.

Betelgeuse

Betelgeuse is a red supergiant star nearing the end of its life, known for its size and brightness.
